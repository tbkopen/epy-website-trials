## Reason clearly about uncertainty

Probability is the mathematics of not knowing — and almost every interesting problem involves not knowing. This course gives you a **rigorous yet intuitive** command of it, from first axioms to modern resampling methods.

Every idea is reinforced with a simulation. You won't just read that the sample mean concentrates — you'll watch it happen and bound how fast.

## Who this is for

- Data scientists who want to move past cookbook statistics
- Researchers who need defensible inference
- Engineers building anything that reasons under uncertainty

## Prerequisites

Single-variable calculus and basic Python.
