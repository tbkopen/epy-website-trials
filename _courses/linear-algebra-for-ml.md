## Linear algebra, the way it should be taught

Most courses drown you in row reduction before you ever see why any of it matters. This one is **geometry first**: you'll *see* what a matrix does to space before you compute with it.

By the end you'll understand the machinery behind PCA, least squares, and the embeddings that power modern ML — not as formulas to memorise, but as ideas you can reason with.

## Who this is for

- Aspiring data scientists and ML practitioners
- Engineers who want the *why* behind the libraries they use
- Anyone who found university linear algebra mechanical and unsatisfying

## Prerequisites

High-school algebra and a little Python. That's genuinely all.
